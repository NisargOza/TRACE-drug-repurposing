Pirfenidone — Honest Limitation Statement
=======================================================

REVERSAL RANK (all arms):
  Baseline (KS):          rank 1251/1768  (70.8%)
  Net-TRACE (net. prop.): rank  842/1768  (47.6%)
  VAE-TRACE:              rank  699/1768  (39.5%)
  Combined model:         rank  793/1768  (44.9%)

WHY PIRFENIDONE DOES NOT RECOVER:
  Pirfenidone's mechanism is antioxidant and diffuse anti-fibrotic (TGF-β1
  pathway attenuation, NFE2L2/Nrf2 activation, PDGF-B suppression). It has
  no specific binding target catalogued in ChEMBL with MOA-level evidence,
  so its L1000 signature does not produce the coherent, directional reversal
  that a target-specific drug would. The method's reversal scoring recovers
  drugs whose L1000 profiles strongly anti-correlate with the IPF signature;
  pirfenidone's diffuse mechanism means its L1000 profile lacks the focused
  anti-correlation TRACE is designed to detect.

WHY THIS IS DEFENSIBLE, NOT A FAILURE:
  A method that only recovers drugs with clean transcriptional reversal signatures
  is being honest about its scope. Pirfenidone is a known edge case in L1000
  analyses precisely because its mechanism is not target-specific. The failure
  to recover pirfenidone does NOT mean the method is wrong; it means the method
  correctly identifies target-specific reversal and pirfenidone is not that kind
  of drug. This limitation is stated explicitly in the report and does not
  undermine the recovery of nintedanib (rank 15, 0.8%) or the novel candidates.

WHAT WAS NOT DONE (and why):
  The approach of manually assigning pirfenidone's targets (TGFB1, FGF2, VEGFA)
  to lift its genetic-support score was reverted. Reason: it special-cases the
  benchmark drug while leaving 1,672 other drugs under-annotated, which is
  inconsistent with the framing of item 2 (circularity fix). The correct path
  is systematic target expansion via item 5 (ChEMBL REST + OT pipeline) for
  ALL drugs, not hand-curated targets for the one drug we want to rank well.
